What is CPF?
The Central Provident Fund (CPF) is Singapore's mandatory savings scheme. Both you and your employer contribute a portion of your monthly salary into CPF. These savings fund three major life needs: retirement, housing, and healthcare.

The three CPF accounts explained
Ordinary Account (OA)
Use for buying a home (HDB flat), paying mortgage, education fees. Earns 2.5% interest per year.
Special Account (SA)
Locked for retirement. Earns 4% per year — higher because you can't touch it until age 55. At 55, becomes your Retirement Account (RA).
MediSave Account (MA)
Pay for hospitalisation, surgery, and MediShield Life premiums. Earns 4% per year.
Key insight: CPF means your employer pays an extra 17% on top of your salary just for your CPF. A $3,500 salary actually costs your employer about $4,095/month. This is how the government ensures workers save for retirement, housing, and healthcare — even if they forget to save themselves.
