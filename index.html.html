CPF System & Housing Affordability in Singapore
💡 What is CPF? The Central Provident Fund is Singapore's mandatory savings system. Every working Singaporean and their employer contribute a % of salary every month into three special accounts — ensuring everyone saves for retirement, housing, and healthcare — even if they forget to!

🗂️ The 3 CPF accounts in detail
🏠
Ordinary Account (OA)
Use for your HDB flat mortgage, education fees, and approved investments. This is where most working Singaporeans fund their home.
2.5% interest p.a.
👴
Special Account (SA)
Locked for retirement. Earns higher interest because you can't withdraw until age 55. At 55 it becomes your Retirement Account (RA).
4% interest p.a.
🏥
MediSave Account (MA)
Pay for hospitalisation, surgery and MediShield Life premiums. Your healthcare safety net when medical bills get large.
4% interest p.a.
🤔 Think about it: Your employer pays an extra 17% on top of your salary just for your CPF. A $3,500 salary actually costs your employer ~$4,095/month. The government designed CPF so that even people who don't think about saving — automatically save anyway.
